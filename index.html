ℹ HOW OIRB WORKS
The Operations & IT Review Board approves all releases with risk score ≥ 60 or OT/SCADA type. Releases in the queue below are awaiting board review. On the meeting day, the chair reviews each release in full, then records Approved, Deferred, or Rejected. Deferred = postponed to next meeting (stays in queue). Rejected = returned to Draft for rework.

ℹ HOW IMPACT IS IDENTIFIED — Dependency Graph
Every CI declares Depends On (upstream — what it consumes) and Consumed By (downstream — what depends on it). When a release is created, GridFlow traces these links to identify Direct Impacts, Indirect Impacts, and Upstream Notices. Keep CI records current to ensure accurate impact analysis and risk scoring.

ℹ HOW COMPLIANCE FLAGS ARE DETECTED
GridFlow auto-detects applicable frameworks when a release is created based on domain and release type. OT/SCADA → NERC CIP + IEC 62351. Transmission → FERC. Billing → SOX + GDPR/PII. Customer-facing → GDPR/PII. Gen or Distribution assets → ISO 55000. Flags can be manually adjusted before submission. Each flag generates mandatory evidence requirements traceable to the audit package.
